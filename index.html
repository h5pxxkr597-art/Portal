About Timothy Syndrome Research Portal

Our Mission

The Timothy Syndrome Research Portal is dedicated to advancing research and treatment for Timothy Syndrome by providing researchers, clinicians, and families with comprehensive access to genetic and clinical data related to CACNA1C and CACNA1D mutations.

What is Timothy Syndrome?

Timothy Syndrome (TS) is an extremely rare genetic disorder caused by mutations in the CACNA1C gene (TS1) or CACNA1D gene (TS2). It affects cardiac function, causing long QT syndrome and life-threatening arrhythmias, and also impacts neurological development, immune function, and other organ systems. The syndrome is characterized by multi-organ dysfunction including autism spectrum features, syndactyly, and immune deficiency.

Database Features

  • Comprehensive variant database for CACNA1C and CACNA1D genes
  • Cardiac and neurological phenotype correlations
  • Clinical significance annotations
  • Long QT interval measurements and outcomes
  • Treatment response data
  • Regular updates from ongoing research
